Answers to
your questions

Does ChurchPay charge fees? +

ChurchPay is free for your church to set up and use. A small fee is taken from each gift to cover card processing — far less than most church management platforms. No monthly fees. No setup fees. No surprises.

How do members give? +

Members can give by scanning a QR code shown during service or by using the ChurchPay app. Setting up recurring tithes takes about ten seconds. Year-end statements are generated automatically — no work from your treasurer.

Is ChurchPay a bank? Are funds FDIC-insured? +

ChurchPay is a financial technology company, not a bank. Funds are held with FDIC-insured partner banks and are eligible for FDIC insurance up to $250,000 per depositor.

Can multiple people manage the church account? +

Yes. Pastors, treasurers, deacons, and administrators can each have their own access with custom permissions. Cards can be issued with spending limits and category controls — so the right people have the right access.

How is ChurchPay different from PayPal or Venmo? +

ChurchPay is purpose-built for churches. It handles designated funds, automatic year-end giving statements, multi-leader card controls, board-ready reports, and cross-border branch transfers — none of which a regular payment app can do.

How long does it take to get set up? +

Most churches go from sign-up to accepting their first gift in under 30 minutes. Setup takes about 15 minutes once you have your church's basic info and a couple of leaders to invite.

I want to talk to someone. How? +

Reach us at hello@churchpay.com or request a quick walkthrough. We usually respond the same day.
